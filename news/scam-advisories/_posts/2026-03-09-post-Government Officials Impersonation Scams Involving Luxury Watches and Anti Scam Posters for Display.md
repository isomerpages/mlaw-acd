<p>Dear Regulated Dealers</p>
<p>We would like to draw your attention to a new variant of Government Officials
Impersonation Scams ("<strong>GOIS</strong>") involving the physical collection
of luxury watches.</p>
<p>In this scam variant, victims would receive unsolicited calls from scammers
impersonating representatives from insurance and financial institutions,
or retailers claiming that the victims had outstanding insurance premiums
or had performed bank card transactions. When victims denied the claims,
they would be transferred to scammers impersonating government officials
from the Ministry of Law or Monetary Authority of Singapore and be accused
of money laundering. Victims would then be instructed to hand over valuables
such as luxury watches to unknown subjects for purported "investigation
purposes". Victims would only realise they have been scammed when the scammers
became uncontactable, or when the victim sought verification from the relevant
agencies.</p>
<p>We would like to remind regulated dealers to remain vigilant against GOIS
and that you play an important role in working with the Police to deter,
detect and prevent crime. To help protect your customers, we encourage
you to display anti-scam infographics (see download links below), developed
by the Police, at appropriate locations in your shopfront or on your website
to raise customer awareness about these scam variants.</p>

<p>We also encourage you to take note of the latest crime statistics in the
<a href="https://www.police.gov.sg/Media-Hub/Police-Life/2026/02/Scams-and-Cybercrime-Fell-by-Almost-a-Quarter-in-2025" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">Annual Scam and Cybercrime Brief 2025</a>, which was released by the Police
on 25 February 2026 as part of their ongoing public education efforts.
The brief provides comprehensive insights into scam trends and prevention
measures to help you stay informed about emerging threats. In particular,
please note the section on GOIS, which highlights the concerning trend
of victims being instructed to hand over valuables such as gold, jewellery,
and luxury watches to scammers.</p>
<p>If you observe any suspicious behaviour, please alert your nearest Neighbourhood
Police Centre. If you have information relating to such crimes or are in
doubt, call the Police Hotline at 1800-255-0000, or submit it online at
<a href="https://www.police.gov.sg/i-witness" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.police.gov.sg/i-witness</a>. All information will be kept
strictly confidential. If you require urgent Police assistance, please
dial ‘999’.</p>
<p>Your vigilance and support in implementing these measures will help protect
our community from these evolving scam threats.</p>
