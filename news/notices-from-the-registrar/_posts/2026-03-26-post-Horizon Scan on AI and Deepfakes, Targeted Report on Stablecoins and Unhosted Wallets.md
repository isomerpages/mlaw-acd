<p>Dear Regulated Dealer,
<br>
<br>The Financial Action Task Force (“<strong>FATF</strong>”) has published
reports on the money laundering, terrorism financing and proliferation
financing (“<strong>ML/TF/PF</strong>”) risks posed by new and emerging
technologies and rising trends in such technologies, in particular, a <strong>Horizon Scan on Artificial Intelligence (“AI”) and Deepfakes, </strong>and
a <strong>Targeted Report on Stablecoins and Unhosted Wallets.</strong>
<br>
<br>The FATF reports highlight how these technologies may be exploited by
illicit actors and underscore the need for regulated entities to remain
vigilant to the risks that these technologies pose.
<br>
<br><strong>What are the rising trends in these technologies?</strong>
<br>
<br>There is a rising trend where <strong>AI enabled deepfakes</strong> (e.g.,
videos, images or audio created using AI techniques) are <strong>now more widely used</strong>,
including to mimic real people’s appearance, voice or actions to impersonate
individuals and/or facilitate fraud and other illicit activities.
<br>
<br><strong>Stablecoins</strong>, including through <strong>unhosted wallets</strong> (i.e.,
wallets that do not involve a virtual asset service provider (“<strong>VASP</strong>”)
or financial institution (“<strong>FI</strong>”) subject to anti-money
laundering, countering financing of terrorism and countering proliferation
financing (“<strong>AML/CFT/CPF</strong>”) obligations), <strong>have increasingly become a common component of ML, TF and PF schemes that use virtual assets (“VAs”)</strong>. <strong>Stablecoins</strong> generally
refer to a type of VA, and can be used as a means of payment and/or store
of value. Stablecoins have a mechanism (e.g., linkage to reference assets
such as fiat currencies or other VAs) with which they purport to maintain
price stability.
<br>
<br><strong>Why are these rising trends a concern?</strong>
<br>
<br>Once rare, <strong>deepfakes </strong>have become increasingly prevalent
and can be used to circumvent AML/CFT/CPF controls, particularly customer
due diligence (“<strong>CDD</strong>”) systems and measures. Deepfakes
can be used to <strong>impersonate individuals and manipulate biometric authentication</strong>,
a concern given a growing reliance on biometric verification. They can
be used to commit ML/TF/PF, and such technologies are also being used in
consumer fraud schemes and phishing attacks. <strong>Generative AI</strong> can
also be used to create fake documents that can facilitate fraud and deception,
including by creating false documentation so that transactions or economic
activities appear real.
<br>
<br>While <strong>stablecoins </strong>have the same vulnerabilities as other
VAs, stablecoins are more likely to be used in peer-to-peer (<strong>“P2P”</strong>)
transactions due to their price stability and ample liquidity. Conducted
without the involvement of AML/CFT/CPF-obliged intermediaries, P2P transactions <strong>via unhosted wallets</strong> are
exposed to heightened ML/TF/PF risk. Reports indicate that stablecoins
are the most popular VA used in illicit transactions, and the FATF has
observed that the <strong>use of stablecoins by illicit actors has continued to increase over time</strong>.
Stablecoins have become increasingly attractive to illicit actors due to
their <strong>liquidity, interoperability and ease of cross-border transfer</strong>.
Illicit actors may collect illicit proceeds in the form of stablecoins
or <strong>convert laundered funds into stablecoins</strong>, before exchanging
them into fiat currency.
<br>
<br><strong>What can you do?</strong>
<br>
<br>Regulated dealers should:</p>
<p>• Keep abreast of evolving cyber risks and threats, relevant advisories,
and ensure your risk mitigation measures, internal controls, staff training,
and technological and system defences are reviewed and enhanced where necessary.
You are encouraged to review how cyber-enabled threats may impact how you
fulfil your AML/CFT/CPF obligations, such as CDD/ enhanced CDD (“<strong>ECDD</strong>”)
measures.</p>
<p>• Regularly review your risk assessment and internal policies, procedures
and controls to align with AML/CFT/CPF requirements and ensure they remain
updated and relevant, addressing risks posed by evolving technology – including
but not limited to GenAI and deepfake technologies.
<br>
<br>Regulated dealers may also refer to earlier papers published by the Monetary
Authority of Singapore (“<strong>MAS</strong>”) concerning cyber risks
associated with <a href="https://www.mas.gov.sg/regulation/circulars/cyber-risks-associated-with-generative-artificial-intelligence" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">generative artificial intelligence (“</a><strong><a href="https://www.mas.gov.sg/regulation/circulars/cyber-risks-associated-with-generative-artificial-intelligence" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">GenAI</a></strong>
<a href="https://www.mas.gov.sg/regulation/circulars/cyber-risks-associated-with-generative-artificial-intelligence" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">”)</a>and <a href="https://www.mas.gov.sg/regulation/circulars/cyber-risks-associated-with-deepfakes" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">deepfakes</a>.
<br>
<br>Regulated dealers are also reminded of:</p>
<p>• The obligation under section 16 of the Precious Stones and Precious
Metals (Prevention of Money Laundering, Terrorism Financing and Proliferation
Financing) Act 2019 (“<strong>PSPM Act</strong>”) to <strong>conduct CDD</strong> before
entering into a designated transaction, when there is reason to suspect
ML/TF/PF, when there is reason to doubt the veracity or adequacy of information
obtained from earlier CDD measures, or under prescribed circumstances.</p>
<p>These prescribed circumstances include the obligation under regulation
4A of the Precious Stones and Precious Metals (Prevention of Money Laundering,
Terrorism Financing and Proliferation Financing) Regulations 2019 (“<strong>PSPM PMLTFPF Regulations</strong>”)
to <strong>conduct CDD</strong> for <strong>payments in digital payment tokens (including stablecoins) exceeding S$20,000</strong>.</p>
<p>• Under regulation 7 of the PSPM PMLTFPF Regulations, regulated dealers
are also required to <strong>conduct ECDD</strong> when they have reason
to believe that a customer, person on whose behalf the customer is acting
or a beneficial owner of the customer, or a transaction may present a <strong>high risk of ML/TF/PF</strong>.
Regulated dealers should carefully assess the risks of transactions involving
payment in stablecoins or other digital payment tokens, conduct CDD and
ECDD where required, and <strong>file a suspicious transaction report (“STR”)</strong> in
a timely manner if circumstances exist that require the regulated dealer
to do so.
